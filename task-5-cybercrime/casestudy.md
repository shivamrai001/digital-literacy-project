Phishing is one of the most common cybercrimes where attackers trick users into revealing sensitive information such as passwords, bank details, or OTPs. 
This crime usually occurs through fake emails, messages, or websites that appear to be from trusted organizations.
In a typical phishing attack, the attacker sends an email pretending to be from a bank or online service. 
The message usually contains urgent language such as “Your account will be blocked” or “Verify your details immediately.” 
The email includes a link that directs the victim to a fake website designed to look like the real one.
When the victim enters login credentials or financial information, the attacker collects the data and can use it to access the victim’s accounts.
Students and young internet users are often targeted because they frequently use online platforms and may not always verify links carefully. 
The consequences of phishing can include financial loss, identity theft, and unauthorized access to personal accounts.
To avoid phishing attacks, users should carefully check email addresses, avoid clicking suspicious links, and never share passwords or OTPs.
Awareness and cautious online behavior are the most effective ways to prevent phishing attacks.
